# Deep learning and LLMs for ride-hailing: matching, pricing, and rebalancing

Ride-hailing research in 2023–2026 has consolidated around **deep reinforcement learning (DRL) and graph neural networks (GNNs)** as the default methodological stack, with a fast-growing **LLM/foundation-model** frontier layered on top. The three operational levers — **order matching**, **dynamic pricing**, and **fleet rebalancing** — are increasingly studied as one coupled system rather than separately, driven by the realization that pricing shapes demand, demand shapes matching feasibility, and matching outcomes determine the spatial distribution of idle supply that rebalancing must correct. The field is mature enough that industrial deployments (Didi, Lyft) now appear in top venues, while open questions concentrate on safe offline learning, fairness, cross-city transfer, and whether LLMs can serve as planners or reward designers rather than ornamental components.

## 1. Field map

The subfield is organized around three tightly interacting control loops on a spatiotemporal graph of a city. **Matching** (who picks up whom, bipartite assignment at short horizons, ≤seconds) feeds **rebalancing** (where idle drivers should go at medium horizons, minutes) because the post-match idle distribution is the state that rebalancing acts on; **pricing** (surge/spatial fares at medium-to-long horizons) modulates both sides of the market, changing request arrival rates and driver self-repositioning. Methodologically the field moved from **single-agent value learning (Didi 2018–19)** to **multi-agent RL with mean-field approximations (2019–22)**, then to **graph-based MARL** where agents are either grid-cells or vehicles and a GNN encodes spatial dependence (2023–25); in parallel a **bi-level GNN-RL + combinatorial-optimization** paradigm (Pavone/Gammelli) dominates autonomous mobility-on-demand (AMoD). Pricing research splits into an **economics/OR track** that pursues equilibrium and mechanism-design results (Castillo, Besbes, Bimpikis, Ma-Parkes) and a **DRL track** that scales actor-critic methods to networks. A new **LLM/foundation-model** thread — urban ST foundation models (UrbanGPT, UniST) plus LLM agents for dispatch/rebalancing (GARLIC, LLM-ODDR) — is the most visible recent shift, alongside growing attention to **joint optimization** of all three levers (JDRCL) and **fairness**.

## 2. Research threads

**Thread A — Production-scale and offline RL for dispatching.** *Question:* How do we learn and safely deploy RL dispatch policies on live platforms with millions of daily trips? *Status:* **Active and maturing.** Two deployed systems (Didi's Eshkevari et al. 2022; Lyft's Azagirre et al. 2024) set the bar, while offline RL (NondBREM, AAAI'24) and end-to-end learned matchers (Yue et al., CIKM'24) aim to eliminate the legacy "value-function + Hungarian" pipeline. *Open questions:* safe exploration under distribution shift; provable bounds for offline RL with logged bandit data; cross-city transfer without per-city retraining.

**Thread B — Graph-based and mean-field MARL for matching and repositioning.** *Question:* How do thousands of driver-agents coordinate without centralized training or intractable joint action spaces? *Status:* **Very active, still growing.** GAT-MF (KDD'23), CoopRide (KDD'25), BMG-Q (T-ITS'25), and constrained mean-field RL (Jusup et al. 2025) are representative; GNN attention over bipartite or region graphs is now the default encoder. *Open questions:* overestimation under decentralized training; non-stationarity; integrating fairness/accessibility as hard constraints.

**Thread C — Autonomous mobility-on-demand (AMoD) control via hybrid learning + optimization.** *Question:* How do we combine the expressiveness of GNN-RL with the feasibility guarantees of classical network-flow optimization for profit-maximizing robo-taxi fleets? *Status:* **Active, methodologically leading.** The Pavone lab's bi-level paradigm (RL proposes target distribution, LP executes) dominates — extended from CDC'21 through ICML'23, ECC'24 (electric AMoD), and 2026 robo-taxi scaling. TUM's hybrid MADRL + bipartite matching is a parallel line (Enders'23, Hoppe'24). *Open questions:* stability/safety guarantees, charging integration, mixed AV + human fleets, cross-city generalization.

**Thread D — Dynamic, spatiotemporal, and surge pricing.** *Question:* How should platforms price in space and time under strategic drivers and riders, and can DRL match or beat analytical equilibrium policies? *Status:* **Saturating on the OR/economics side, active on the DRL side.** Castillo's Econometrica 2025 closes a canonical question on the welfare incidence of surge, building on the Besbes-Castro-Lobel spatial-pricing line. Joint pricing+matching DRL (Zhang-Varakantham AAAI'23) is newer but not yet standardized. *Open questions:* bridging deep demand forecasters with equilibrium pricing; mean-field RL for pricing; fairness-efficiency tradeoffs; pricing under platform competition and regulation.

**Thread E — LLMs, foundation models, and joint three-way optimization.** *Question:* Can LLMs/foundation models plan, price, dispatch, and rebalance — or at least serve as simulators, reward designers, and cross-task transfer substrates? *Status:* **Emerging, rapidly growing.** Urban ST foundation models (UrbanGPT KDD'24, UniST KDD'24, UrbanDiT) are already active; LLM-as-controller for ride-hailing specifically (GARLIC AAAI'25, LLM-ODDR 2025, DiMA at Didi) is brand new, mostly arXiv/first-year conference. Joint matching+pricing+repositioning DRL remains rare, with JDRCL a lone peer-reviewed beachhead. *Open questions:* grounding and hallucination in quantitative decisions; distilling LLM reasoning into low-latency policies; theoretical convergence of three-way joint DRL; integrating strategic agents (IRL + equilibrium).

## 5. Terminology note

Search coverage should include the following **synonym clusters**; papers often pick one framing and ignore the others even when methods are identical.

For the **problem domain**, "ride-hailing" and "ride-sharing" are frequently interchanged in ML venues but mean different things in OR literature: ride-hailing = solo trips (Uber/Didi Express), ride-sharing = shared/pooled (UberPool, ride-pooling). "**Mobility-on-demand (MoD)**" is the umbrella term; "**autonomous mobility-on-demand (AMoD)**" denotes robo-taxi fleets and dominates the Pavone-lab literature. Related classical-OR terms include "**dynamic vehicle routing problem (DVRP)**", "**dial-a-ride problem (DARP)**", and "**stochastic dynamic pickup-and-delivery**".

For **matching**, search under "**order dispatching**" (Didi-style), "**order assignment**", "**passenger-driver assignment**", "**taxi dispatch**", "**bipartite assignment / bipartite matching**", "**online matching**", and for pooled settings "**trip-vehicle assignment**" or "**ride-pooling assignment**". The term "**anticipatory assignment**" signals a forward-looking value-function formulation.

For **rebalancing**, the literature uses "**vehicle repositioning**", "**fleet rebalancing**", "**idle driver guidance / relocation / recommendation**", "**empty vehicle routing**", "**anticipatory routing**", and in AV literature "**fleet coordination**" or "**robo-taxi dispatch**". "**Driver guidance**" is common in Didi papers; "**rebalancing**" is the default in AMoD papers.

For **pricing**, terms include "**dynamic pricing**", "**surge pricing**", "**spatial pricing**", "**spatiotemporal pricing (STP)**", "**origin-based pricing**", "**driver surge / additive surge**", and "**two-sided market pricing**". Mechanism-design framings use "**welfare-optimal pricing**" and "**core-selecting**".

For **methods**, "**DRL**" covers DQN, DDPG, TD3, SAC, PPO; "**MARL**" (multi-agent RL) includes IDQN, VDN, QMIX, MAPPO, MASAC; "**mean-field RL (MFRL) / mean-field MARL (MF-MARL) / mean-field control (MFC)**" are close cousins distinguished by solution concept. "**GNN**" is used interchangeably with "**graph attention network (GAT)**", "**spatiotemporal graph neural network (STGNN)**", "**graph convolutional network (GCN)**", and when combined with RL, "**graph RL (GRL)**". "**Foundation model for mobility / urban ST foundation model / spatiotemporal LLM**" are overlapping labels for the UrbanGPT/UniST class. Finally, "**hybrid learning + optimization**" or "**decision-focused learning**" describes the bi-level RL + LP/ILP approach dominant in AMoD.

## Conclusion

The ride-hailing ML subfield has crossed from exploratory DRL into a mature engineering discipline with deployed systems at Didi and Lyft and standardized MARL + GNN baselines. The economic/OR track on surge pricing is visibly saturating as canonical questions get resolved in MS and Econometrica. The **genuinely open frontier** lies in three places: **(i)** LLM/foundation-model agents that reason over spatiotemporal state and may act as reward designers, simulators, or meta-controllers — currently emerging with only a handful of peer-reviewed papers; **(ii)** **true three-way joint optimization** of pricing + matching + rebalancing with theoretical guarantees, where JDRCL is a lone peer-reviewed beachhead; and **(iii)** **fairness, safety, and offline learning** as first-class objectives rather than post-hoc constraints. A reader entering the field today should anchor on the Qin et al. 2022 TR-C survey for taxonomy, the Gammelli-Pavone GNN-RL line for AMoD methodology, the Castillo / Besbes-Castro-Lobel work for pricing theory, and the UrbanGPT/UniST/GARLIC cluster to track where LLMs are going next.
